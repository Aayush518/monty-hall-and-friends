What is the Monty Hall Problem?

The Monty Hall problem is a famous probability puzzle based on a game show scenario. Here's how it works:

  1. There are three doors. Behind one door is a prize (a car), and behind the other two are goats.
  2. You pick a door, but it remains closed.
  3. The host, who knows what's behind the doors, opens another door, revealing a goat.
  4. The host then asks if you want to switch your choice to the remaining unopened door or stick with your original choice.

The question is: Should you switch or stay with your original choice?

Counterintuitively, you have a better chance of winning if you switch. The probability of winning by switching is 2/3, while the probability of winning by staying is 1/3.
